Francesco Limberti

I'm Francesco Limberti — SisQo to this site. I started writing code at 8 years old and never really stopped; three decades later it's still web projects, one deploy (and the occasional 2am debugging session) at a time.

As a developer, I work full-stack — Java, Spring Boot, MySQL, Redis, and Docker, running on cloud infrastructure I'm comfortable owning end to end, with years of e-commerce at real scale behind me. Clean Code by Robert C. Martin has stuck with me as the core reference for how I reason about software: readability over cleverness, always. But clean code is the starting point, not the goal — what I care about is architecture that holds up, systems that stay fast and reliable under load, and code that reaches production. I have strong opinions about everything in between, and a Git history to prove it.

Somewhere along the way I stopped just writing code for other people's ideas and started building my own. As a founder and CTO, I've built and led an e-commerce company from zero — steering both the technology and the business direction. What pulls me in is the same thing every time: a real problem worth solving, the chance to turn an idea into a working product, and the shot at building something that reaches people at scale. The engineering is the easy part; making it matter is the work.

More recently that work runs on AI. I integrate LLMs into products that live in production and build agents and automations that do jobs that matter — mostly on top of the major model APIs, with tool use and MCP-style agents where they earn their place. My take on AI is deliberately pragmatic: it's a genuinely powerful tool, best pointed at problems where it creates real value, and mostly ignored where it's just noise. I'd rather ship something useful with it than talk about it.

Three roles, one habit: I like building things that work, and then making them work better.
